## Peroxisome
A peroxisome is a membraneous organelle that contains vesicles (which are their own membranous sacs) that contain degradative enzymes.  Peroxisomes are used to break down organic compounds and neutralize the toxic compounds generated in the process.

## Lyosome
Lysosomes also contain vesicles that have digestive enzymes.  They are used to break down organic compounds and damaged organelles/pathogens (evil guys). (dark horse mvp of the human body)

## Microvilli
Microvilli are membrane extensions that contain microfilaments.  Their function is to increase surface area of the cell to better absorb extracellular materials.

## Golgi apparatus
The Golgi apparatus is made up of stacks of flattened membranes called cisternae that contain chambers.  It stores, alters, and packages proteins (post synthesis).

## Nucleus
The nucleus contains fluid nucleoplasm that contains enzymes, proteins, DNA, and nucleotides.  It's surrounded by a double membrane called the nuclear envelope.  It controls the metabolism, stores and processes genetic info, and it also controls protein synthesis.

## Endoplasmic reticulum
The structure of the er is a network of membranous sheets and channels that extend throughout the cytoplasm.  The ER's function is synthesis of secretory products, intracellular storage and transport, and also the detoxification of drugs or toxins.

The smooth er has no attached ribosomes and it synthesizes lipids and carbohydrates.  The rough er has ribosomes bound to the membranes and it modifies and packages newly synthesized proteins.

##  Ribosomes
Ribosomes are made up of RNA and protein.  There are fixed ribosomes which are bound to the rough er and free ones which are scattered in the cytoplasm.

## Mitochondrion
The mitochondrion are surrounded by a double membrane, with the inner membrane's folds surrounding important metabolic enzymes.  It producces 95% of the ATP required by the cell wow. 

## Cytoskeleton
Made up of proteins organized into fine filaments (microfilaments) or slender tubes (microtubules) with an organizing center at the centrosome, which is a cytoplasmic region that has a pair of centrioles.  They strengthen and suppor the cell and aid in the movement of cellular structures and materials.
