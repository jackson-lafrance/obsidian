## How are key nutrient compounds made
During the process of [[Metabolism overview|anabolism]], glucose, fatty acids, and amino acids, are used to synthesize a large number of more complex body proteins, lipids, carbs, and other compounds.  We also have the ability to synthesize all of these guys.

### Gluconeogenesis is the synthesis of glucose
If we don't have enough carbs we can make new ones from noncarbohydrate substrates with a process called gluconeogenesis.  The glucogenic [[Amino acids and peptides|amino acids]] are the main substrates we use for this, especially the ones floating around in our blood.  A small amount can also be made from the glycerol in [[Triglycerides|triglycerides]].  The body relies on this to keep blood glucose levels stable at night when we are sleeping/fasting or when rates of [[Carbohydrate metabolism|glucose oxidation]] are high like in trauma or exercise.

### Lipogenesis is the synthesis of fatty acids
Lipogenesis is the production of fat from non fat substances like carbs, ketogenic amino acids, and alcohol.  Its also called **de novo synthesis**.  It usually happens when we consume excess macronutrients than we need and usually happens in liver cells.  It needs a lot of steps.

### Cholesterol is made from acetyl CoA
The liver and intestine make more [[Phospholipids and sterols|cholesterol]] than most people have in their diet in total.  Its made from acetyl CoA which is made during the [[Fat metabolism|metabolism of fatty acids]], alcohol, ketogenic amino acids, and through pyruvate, glucose.  Its used in the production of bile acids, steroid hormones, and vitamin D.  Its also an essential part of all [[Cell structure|cell membranes]].

### Transamination allows synthesis of nonessential amino acids
When making any of the 11 nonessential guys, the body usually makes the carbon skeleton from a carbohydrate.  The amine group gets provided through transamination, where it is donated by one amino and accepted by a keto acids to make a new amino acid.
