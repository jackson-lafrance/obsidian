## Cell structure
- **Plasma membrane** (cell membrane) splits the extracellular fluid from the inner contents of the cell
	- **Cytoplasm** is the filling inside the cell.  Kind of like a jell, it is a colloid (heterogeneous mixture that suspends items of the cell) with a consistency that is kind of like maple syrup
		- **Cytosol** is the fluid part of cytoplasm and its also called intracellular fluid.  It may contain insoluble materials called inclusions
		- **[[Organelles]]** are the intracellular structures (the guys) suspended within the cytosol and they all have specific functions
			- **Nonmembranous organelles** are not enclosed by their own membranes, and all of their components are contacting the cytosol directly.  These include
				- Cytoskeleton
				- Microvilli
				- Centrioles
				- Cilia
				- Flagella
				- Ribosomes
			- **Membranous organelles** are surrounded by membranes that prevent their inner contents from contacting the cytosol ([[Phospholipids and sterols|phospholipid]] membranes).  These include
				- Mitochondria
				- Golgi apparatus
				- Nucleus
				- Endoplasmic reticulum
				- Lysosomes
				- Peroxisomes
