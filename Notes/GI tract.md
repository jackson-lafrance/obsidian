When we eat, the food we consume gets digested, then the nutrients are absorbed, and finally, the waste products are eliminated.

- **Digestion** is the process by which foods are broken down into their component molecules, either mechanically or chemically.
- **Absorption** is the process where these products of digestion move through the wall of the intestine
- **Elimination** is the process where undigested parts of the food and waste products get removed from the body.

These processes occur in the gastrointestinal tract.  Gastro means stomach and intestinal means the small and large intestines.  But the GI tract really starts at the mouth and ends at the anus.  Its a long continuous tube made up of four major layers.

## Layers of the GI tract
- The **mucosa**, the innermost layer surrounds an interior space called the **lumen**. The mucosa is made up of 3 separate tissues and has **goblet cells** that secrete mucus, which protects the GI tract from damage from food hitting it.
- The **submucosa** is made of connective tissue and has a network of nerves that help regulate the GI tract function.
- The **muscularis externa** is the main muscle of the GI tract.  It has a sheet of *circular muscle* that squeezes food in the tract and a sheet of *longtiduinal muscle* that propels food along.  It also has a network of nerves that controls these contractions.
- The **serosa**, the outermost layer, separates the GI tract from the overlying peritoneum of the abdominal cavity

The organs of the GI tract are the mouth, pharynx, esophagus, stomach, small intestine, and large intestine.  The flow of food between them is controlled by **sphincters**, which are tiny rings of muscle that open when a nerve lets them know that food is ready to be passed into the next section.  

There are also several accessory organs of digestion, including salivary glands, liver, pancreas, gallbladder, which each have a role in digesting and absorbing nutrients.

## Organs of the GI tract
### [[Mouth]]
**Ingestion**: Food enters the GI tract through the mouth
**Mechanical Digestion**: Mastication tears, shreds, and mixed food with saliva
**Chemical Digestion**: Salivary amylase and lingual lipase start digesting [[Carbohydrate digestion|carbs]] and [[Lipid digestion|lipids]]

#### Salivary glands
Produce saliva, which is a mixture of water, mucus, enzymes, and other chemicals

### [[Pharynx and esophagus]]
**Propulsion**: Swallowing and peristalsis move the food from mouth to stomach

### [[Stomach]]
**Mechanical digestion**: Mixes and churns food with gastric juices into chyme (liquid)
**Chemical digestion**: Pepsin starts digesting [[Protein digestion|proteins]] and gastric lipase keeps breaking lipids apart.
**Absorption**: Some fat soluble guys go through the stomachs wall

### [[Intestines|Small Intestine]]
**Mechanical digestion**:  Segmentation mixes chyme with digestive juices
**Propulsion**: Peristaltic waves move the chyme along the tract
**Chemical digestion**: Pancreas and brush border give digestive enzymes that digest most nutrients
**Absorption**: Nutrients get [[Nutrient absorption and transport|absorbed into blood and lymph]] through the enterocytes

#### Liver
Produces bile to emulsify fats

#### Pancreas
Stores bile before it releases it into the small intestine through the bile duct

### [[Intestines|Large Intestine]]
**Chemical digestion**: Some leftover food gets digested by bacteria
**Absorption**: Salts, water, and vitamins get reabsorbed
**Propulsion**: Compacts waste into feces and sends it towards the rectum

### Rectum
**Elimination**: Temp stores feces until its time to go (shitted out the anus)
