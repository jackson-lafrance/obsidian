## Most digestion happens in the small intestine
The small intestine is the longest part of the [[GI tract]], but its only an inch in diameter.  It is made up of three key sections.
- The **Duodenum** is the section of the small intestine that is connected to the pyloric sphincter which is connected to the [[Stomach|stomach]]
- The **jejunum** is the middle part
- The **ileum** is the end part that connects to the large intestine through the sphincter called the **ileocecal valve**

Here [[Carbohydrate digestion|carbs]], [[Lipid digestion|lipids]], and [[Protein digestion|proteins]] are broken down into their smallest components and digestion and absorption are achieved through the actions of enzymes and accessory organs (pancreas, gallbladder, and liver).  Once digestion and absorption are completed, the residue is sent to the large intestine.

## The large intestine stores food waste until it is excreted
The large intestine is a thick tubelike structure that frames the small intestine on three and one half sides.  It starts with a tissue sac called the **cecum**.  From there, the large intestine continues up along the right side of the small intestine as the **ascending colon**, across the top as the **transverse colon**, and then the **descending colon** comes down on the left.  These regions are characterized by **haustra**, which are saclike segmentations that contract to move food towards the **sigmoid colon** extends from the bottom left corner to the rectum.

There are lots of bacterial cells called **GI flora** that live in the large intestine, where they finish digesting some of the nutrients remaining in food residue.  The by-products of this are reabsorbed into the body, where they return to the liver and are either stored or used as needed.  Also the GI flora ferments fibrous wastes, making some vitamins that are absorbed by the large intestine.  The GI flora also stop harmful bacteria from growing.  They are also called **brobiotics**.  

No other digestion happens in the large intestine.  But it stores the digestive mass for 12 -> 24 hours, absorbing water, lipids breakdown guys, and electrolytes leaving feces.  Peristalsis occurs a little bit to move feces through the colon, except for when you are trying to shit then its a super big peristalsis which powerfully forces the feces towards the rectum.
