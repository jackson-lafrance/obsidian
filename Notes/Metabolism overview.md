## Why is metabolism essential for life
Metabolism is the sum of all chemical and physical processes by which he body breaks down and builds up molecules.  When nutrition researchers burn food in a calorimeter, carbon dioxide, water, chemical, and thermal energy are released.  In the same way the body uses food for fuel to produce energy.  

## Anabolism and Catabolism require or release energy
The end products of digestion are absorbed from the small [[Intestines|intestine]] and circulated into body's cells.  There they can be broken down more for energy or combined to synthesize compounds like glycogen, cholesterol, hormones, enzymes, or cell membranes.  The process of making larger complex molecules from small guys is called **anabolism**.  It is a process that is necessary for growth, repair, and maintenance of the body's tissues.  These reactions need chemical energy to happen.

**Catabolism** is the breakdown of larger molecules into smaller ones.  It releases chemical energy and is the opposite of anabolism.  Catabolism of food starts with digestion, when chemical reactions break down [[Protein basics|proteins]], [[Lipid basics|lipids]], and [[Carbohydrate basics|carbohydrates]] into end products like amino acids, fatty acids, glycerol, and monosaccharides.  

After absorption, these basic compounds are transported to cells which can break them down further when they need energy.  Catabolism is also used to break down old cells/tissues that need to repaired/replaced.  The energy gained from these reactions is used to fuel our body and build new compounds with anabolism.

A balance between the two maintains health and function but sometimes one of these two processes dominates.  Fetal and childhood is an anabolic state but disease is catabolic.

## Energy stored in ATP fuels the work of all body cells
When cells catabolize glucose, they package and release the energy as [[Mitochondria and ATP|ATP]] (adenosine triphosphate).  The bonds between the three phosphate groups store a lot of potential energy and these bonds are broken whenever a cell needs energy.
