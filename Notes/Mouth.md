## Digestion starts in the mouth
Before you even eat, digestion starts, this is called the **cephalic phase**.  [[Appetite and hunger|Hunger and appetite]] work together to prepare the [[GI tract]] to digest food.  The nervous system stimulates the release of digestive juices preparing for food to enter the GI tract and sometimes can cause hunger pains.

Once you take a bite, chewing moistens the food and breaks it down.  Hormones and other substances get secreted throughout the GI tract once you start chewing. The digestive juices of the mouth (**saliva**) are exposed to food.

## Taste and smell
Taste comes from the chemical dissolved in saliva binding to the chemoreceptors (**taste receptors**) located on the tong on the **taste buds**.  This depends a lot on sense of smell called **olfaction**.  To get olfaction, odorants that get dissolved in mucus bind to the chemoreceptors in the nasal cavity that are called **olfactory receptor cells**.  These cells then transmit their data to the olfactory bulb of the brain.

## Saliva
Saliva also starts the chemical [[Carbohydrate digestion|digestion of carbs]].  It does this with **salivary amylase**.  **Lingual lipase** secreted by tongue cells also starts the [[Lipid digestion|digestion of lipids]].

Saliva contains lots of other components including:
- Bicarbonate - which helps neutralize acids
- Mucus - which moistens food
- Antibodies - proteins that fight bacteria coming into the mouth
- Lysozyme - Enzyme that stops bacteria growth in the mouth
