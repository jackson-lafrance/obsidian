Nucleic acids are large organic molecules made up of C, H, O, N, and P.  The two classes are deoxyribonucleic acid (DNA) and ribonucleic acid (RNA).  The main role of nucleic acid is to store and transfer information, specifically information related to [[Amino acids and peptides|protein synthesis]].  A nucleic acid is a polymer that is made up of one or two long chains of subunits that are made by **dehydration synthesis**.  The individual monomers of a nucleic acid are called nucleotides.

A **DNA** molecule is made up of a pair of nucleotide chains.  Hydrogen bonding between opposing nitrogenous bases holds the two strands together.  The shapes of them allow A to bond only to T and C to bond only to G (**complementary base pairs**).  The two nucleotide chains of DNA are **complementary strands**.

An **RNA** molecule is made up of only one chain of nucleotides.  Its shape and function depends on the order of the nucleotides and the interactions among them. We have three types of RNA in our cells:
- Messanger RNA (mRNA)
- Transfer RNA (tRNA)
- ribosomal RNA (rRNA)
