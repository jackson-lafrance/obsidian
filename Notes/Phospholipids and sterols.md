## Phospholipids combine lipids with phosphate
Phospholipids are abundant in egg yolks, peanuts, and soybeans.  They also have a glycerol backbone and fatty acids at the first two carbons, but the third one gets attached with another compound that contains phosphate.  Since phosphate is soluble in water, so are phospholipids.  This lets them transport fats in the bloodstream.

Phospholipids are special since they have one end that is **hydrophobic** (lipid tail) and one that is **hydrophilic** (phosphate head).  In a [[Cell structure|cell membrane]], this power lets them control the transport of substances in and out of the cell.  Phospholipids also help with digestion of dietary fats.  In the liver **lecithins** mix with bile salts and electrolytes to make bile.  This emulsifies lipids.  Our body creates phospholipids so we don't need to eat them.

## Sterols have a ring structure
Sterols are a type of lipid with a multiple-ring structure and are very different from triglycerides or phospholipids.  Plant foods have some trash sterols, but they block the absorption of dietary cholesterol, which helps regulate it in our diet and lower our levels if they are too high.

**Cholesterol** is found in animal based foods as fatty acid attached to a ring structure.  It is everywhere in the fatty part of animal products like butter, egg yolks, whole milk, meats, and poultry.

You don't need to consume it because all cells can synthesize it.  Most of our internally produced cholesterol production happens in the liver.  Cholesterol is used in the structure of every cell membrane where it works with fatty acids and phospholipids to maintain integrity.  Even though it has a bad reputation it is necessary for human health.
