## What is probability theory
The branch of math focused on the analysis of random stuff.  The most important questions of life are mostly just problems of probability.

**Statistics** starts with data and tells us what seems to happen to estimate long run averages.

**Probability theory** starts with assumptions and tells us what should happen to describe long run averages.

Probability is a value between 0 and 1 that reps the likelihood something happens.

## How to interpret probability
### Definitions
Starts with an **experiment**, which is where the observation/measurement is obtained where the outcome is not known beforehand.

Like tossing a die or picking a card from a deck.

**Simple events** are the outcomes that are observed on a single repetition of the experiment.  Only one simple event can happen each time.  Each simple even will get a probability, which is how likely it is to occur.  It is denoted by *E* with a subscript.

**Sample space**, *S* is the set of all simple events possible in an experiment.
Toss a die S = {1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6}

An **event** is a collection of one more more **simple events**.
Die toss -> Event(an odd number)(a number > 2) (a grouping of guys)

### Assigning probabilities
There are two ways to assign a probability to a **simple event**.  
1. Subjective, aka what seems reasonable to me, what you get told the probability is, what you and someone else agree on (betting)
2. Frequentist (long run results), aka repeating the experiment many times where the probability of something is **its frequency**
		$$ P(A) = \lim_{n\to \infty} (occurances/trials) $$

The probability of an **event** is found by adding the probabilities of all of the **simple events** it contains.
