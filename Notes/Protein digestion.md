## Stomach acids and enzymes break proteins into short polypeptides
Basically no enzymatic digestions of proteins occurs in the mouth.  Once the proteins reach the stomach, hydrochloric acid denatures the protein strands and converts the inactive enzyme **pepsinogen** into **pepsin**, which is the protein-digesting enzyme.  Although pepsin itself is a protein, it doesn't get denatured by the acid since it has evolved.

The hormone **gastrin** controls both production of hydrochloric acid and the release of pepsin.  Thinking about food or chewing it stimulates the gastrin producing cells in the stomach.  Pepsin breaks proteins into single amino acids and shorter polypeptides with **hydrolysis**, they then go to the small intestine for further digestion and absorption.

## Enzymes in the small intestine break polypeptides into single amino acids
When the polypeptides reach the small intestine, the pancreas and small intestine excrete **proteases**, which digest polypeptides.  The types of proteases include trypsin, chymotrypsin, and carboxypeptidase.

The [[Nutrient absorption and transport|enterocytes]] of the small intestine then absorb the single amino acids, dipeptides, and tripeptides.  **Peptidases**, enzymes in the enterocytes break down the dipeptides and tripeptides into single amino acids.  Dipeptidases for breaking dipeptide bonds and tripeptidases for breaking tripeptide bonds.  The amino acids are then transported through the portal vein to the liver where they can be converted to glucose, fat, combined to make new proteins, used for energy, or released into the bloodstream and taken to other cells.

Enterocytes have different sites that specialize in certain types of amino acids, dipeptides, or tripeptides which can effect people taking amino acid supplements.  When large doses of supplements of single amino acids are taken on an empty stomach they compete for the same absorption sites which can block the absorption of other amino acids and lead to deficiencies. So be careful.

## Protein digestibility affects their quality
A few methods are used to estimate a food's protein quality.  One way is a **chemical score**.  This is the comparison of the amount of the limiting amino acid in a food to the amount of the same amino acid in a reference food.  The amino acid with the lowest proportion to the test food is referred to as the limiting amino acid.  This score gives an indication of the lowest amino acid ratio calculated for any amino acid in a particular food.

Another protein quality factor is digestibility.  The PDCAAS uses chemical score and a correction factor for digestibility to calculate protein quality.  Higher digestibility means more complete.  Animal source are highly digestible as are many soy and legume sources.  Grains and vegetables are less :(.  These measures are impractical for diet planning.
