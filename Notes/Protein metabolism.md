## How is energy extracted from proteins
Body's main priority with [[Protein basics|proteins]] is to build and repair body tissues but they can be used for energy in small amounts.  This happens through **proteolysis** where proteins get turned into [[Amino acids and peptides|amino acids]] and transported to the liver where they can be made into proteins again or sent out to cells to build/repair.  If it is consumed in excess of what is needed it can be used for energy or turned into fatty acids.

When we are starved our bodies remove the amino group from a protein with a process called **deamination** which removes the NH2 and leaves a carbon skeleton, called a keto acid.

After deamination the carbon skeleton can be channeled into [[Carbohydrate metabolism|glycolysis]] or the TCA pathway to make energy.  There are two types either glucogenic or ketogenic.

Excess protein is stored as fat, so even though u might think its important to have a lot of protein it just turns into fat in excess.
