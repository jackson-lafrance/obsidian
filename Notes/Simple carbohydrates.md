## Simple carbs
Simple carbs are usually called sugars.  Four of them are called **monosaccharides** because they consist of only one sugar molecule.  Other three are **disaccharides**, which are two sugar molecules joined together.

### Monosaccharides
**Glucose**, **fructose**, and **galactose** are the three most common monosaccharides.  They each have 6 carbon, 12 hydrogen, and 6 oxygen atoms.  Small diffs in the structure of these three cause crazy differences in their level of sweetness.

#### Fructose
The sweetest natural sugar, occurs in fruits and vegetables.  Fructose is also called *levulose* or *fruit sugar*.  In many processed foods, its a big part of high fructose corn syrup.  This is made from corn and is used to sweeten drinks, desserts, candies, and jellies.

#### Galactose
This one doesn't occur alone in foods.  It joins with glucose to create **lactose**, one of the three most common disaccharides.

#### Ribose
Ribose is a 5 carbon monosaccharide.  There isn't alot of it in our diet, our bodies produce it from the foods that we eat and it is contained in ribonucleic acid (RNA), which is a part of the genetic material of cells.

### Disaccharides
The three most common disaccharides found in foods are **lactose**, **maltose**, and **sucrose**.  The two monosaccharides that compose a disaccharide are attached by a bond between oxygen and one carbon from each of the monosaccharides.  Two forms occur in nature **alpha** and **beta** bonds.  Alpha bonds are easily digestible while beta bonds are not.

#### Lactose (milk sugar) - beta bonds
Contains one glucose molecule and one galactose molecule.  Human breast milk has a higher amount of lactose than cow's, which makes it sweeter.  Lactose in dairy products comes from plants.  Cows, sheep, and goats produce lactose from the plants they eat.

#### Maltose (malt sugar)
Two molecules of glucose.  Does not generally occur in foods by itself but is bound together with other molecules.  When we break down larger molecules, maltose is released as a by-product.  Its also the sugar that results from fermentation when alcoholic beverages are produced.

#### Sucrose - alpha bonds
It is composed of one glucose molecule and one fructose.  Since sucrose contains fructose, its sweeter than lactose or maltose.  Sucrose gives most of the sweetness found in sugar, honey, maple syrup, fruits, and veggies.
