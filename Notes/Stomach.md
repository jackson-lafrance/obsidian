## Stomach basics
The stomach is shaped like a J and it can vary in size person to person.  The mucosa and submucosa of the stomach are crinkled into big folds called **rugae**, which flatten progressively to accommodate food.  This lets the stomach expand to hold a full gallon of food/liquid.  When food goes into the small [[Intestines|intestine]], the rugae reform and the stomach shrinks again.

Before any food gets to the stomach, the brain lets it know to prepare to receive food.  For example the hormone **gastrin** is released and stimulates gastric glands to secrete **gastric juice**.  These glands are lined with two important cells:
- **Partietal cells**, which secrete **HCl acid** and **intrinsic factor**, which is a protein required to absorb B vitamins
- **Chief cells**, which secrete **pepsinogen** and **gastric lipase**
	- Pepsiongen is an inactive enzyme that converts to **pepsin** when it touches HCL.  It has to be secreted inactively or else it would just eat its own cells that produce it.  The pepsin is used to [[Protein digestion|digest protein]]. 
	- Gastric lipase is an enzyme that is active and used to [[Lipid digestion|digest lipids]]. They don't really do too much though.

## Stomach digestion and absorption
The stomach also does some mechanical digestion by mixing and churning food around with the gastric juice until it is all a liquid called chyme.  Even though gastric juice is acidic, the stomach doesn't erode because **mucous neck cells** in the gastric glands and **mucous surface cells** in the stomach lining secrete a protective layer of mucus.  

Although most [[Nutrient absorption and transport|absorption]] happens in the small intestine, some stuff gets absorbed through the stomach lining and into the blood.  Like water, fluoride, some lipids, and some lipid soluble drugs like aspirin and alcohol.

Another one of the stomachs jobs is to store the chyme while the small intestine gets ready.  Since the stomach is huge and can store a lot of chyme, if this all went into the small intestine at once it would get cooked.  Chyme stays in the stomach for like two hours before it gets released in spurts into the duodenum.  This release is regulated by the **pyloric sphincter**.
